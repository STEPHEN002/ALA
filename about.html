Our Story

Who We Are

Absterge Lacrimas Africa Uganda Limited is a community-based non-governmental organisation incorporated in Uganda in 2026. The name Absterge Lacrimas is derived from Latin words meaning "wipe away the tears", reflecting our unwavering dedication to restoring hope, dignity, and opportunity among communities facing hardship and vulnerability.

The idea of establishing this organisation was inspired by the long-lasting social, economic, and psychological impacts of armed conflict that affected Northern Uganda for over two decades. Although the war ended many years ago, its effects continue to be felt — thousands of children were deprived of quality education, families lost their livelihoods, and women and youth continue to struggle with poverty and trauma.

As individuals who witnessed these challenges first-hand, we recognised the urgent need for a locally driven organisation committed to rebuilding lives and creating opportunities for lasting transformation.

Purpose

Vision, Mission & Goal

🌍

Vision

An inclusive and empowered Africa where children, youth, and women thrive in dignity, education, health, opportunity, and environmental sustainability.

🎯

Mission

To improve the wellbeing of vulnerable communities in Uganda through integrated, sustainable interventions in education, livelihoods, health, and environmental stewardship — focusing on children, youth, and women as drivers of lasting change.

🏆

Organisational Goal

To improve the overall wellbeing and socio-economic empowerment of vulnerable communities through integrated, sustainable interventions ensuring children, youth, women, and marginalised groups reach their full potential.

Objectives

What We Set Out To Do

Six clear objectives guide all our programs and activities.

01

Reduce child poverty and vulnerability by promoting access to inclusive, quality education, child sponsorship, school-based agricultural learning (AgriSTEM), and strengthened child protection systems.

02

Empower youth and women with vocational skills, entrepreneurship capacity, financial literacy, and leadership competencies for sustainable livelihoods and active community participation.

03

Improve community health outcomes through HIV/AIDS awareness and prevention, sexual and reproductive health education, maternal and child health support, mental health, nutrition, WASH, and psychosocial services.

04

Enhance environmental sustainability and community resilience through climate-smart agriculture, food security initiatives, natural resource conservation, and climate change adaptation strategies.

05

Promote inclusion, equal opportunities, and improved quality of life for persons with disabilities through access to inclusive education, livelihood opportunities, and strengthened community-based protection systems.

06

Generate evidence-based solutions and strengthen program effectiveness through research, data collection, innovation, and knowledge sharing that informs policy and supports sustainable community development.
